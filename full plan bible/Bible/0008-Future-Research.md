## Purpose

Future Research defines the long-term research agenda for AIOS. It catalogs open questions, proposed RFCs, emerging technologies, and speculative directions that may shape future versions of the system. This document is intentionally forward-looking — some topics may never be implemented, but all are worth exploring.

## Horizon Scanning

### Short-Term Horizon (1–2 years)

- **Quantum-safe cryptography migration**: NIST-standardized algorithms (CRYSTALS-Dilithium, CRYSTALS-Kyber) ready for production. AIOS must prepare hybrid cryptography support.
- **Formal verification tools**: Maturation of formal verification for distributed systems. Potential for Security Kernel formal verification.
- **Confidential computing**: Hardware-based trusted execution environments (TEEs) maturing. Potential for enhanced Worker isolation.

### Medium-Term Horizon (2–5 years)

- **Quantum computing threat**: Sufficiently large quantum computers may threaten 2048-bit RSA. AIOS must complete quantum-safe migration before this point.
- **Federated identity standards**: Industry standards for decentralized identity (DID, Verifiable Credentials) may influence AIOS cross-instance identity.
- **AI governance regulation**: Government regulations around AI governance may require AIOS to demonstrate constitutional compliance to external auditors.

### Long-Term Horizon (5–10 years)

- **Post-quantum world**: All classical cryptography replaced by quantum-safe alternatives. AIOS must maintain cryptographic agility.
- **Multi-instance internet**: Thousands of AIOS instances forming a global federated network. Instance discovery and governance at internet scale.
- **Generational AIOS**: Successive AIOS generations with accumulated knowledge and evolved constitutions. Cross-generation knowledge transfer.

## Emerging Technology Impacts

### Consensus Mechanisms

Blockchain-inspired consensus may influence cross-instance governance:
- Event Store replication (Raft-based, not blockchain)
- Cross-instance evidence verification (no global state required)
- Instance-level attestation (not proof-of-work)

### Large Language Models

LLMs may integrate with AIOS in several ways:
- Natural language interface for Human→AIOS interaction
- Sou strategy formulation assistance
- Academy knowledge extraction from unstructured text
- Worker skill enhancement through LLM capabilities

### Edge Computing

Edge deployment of AIOS instances may require:
- Reduced resource footprint for Workers
- Intermittent connectivity tolerance
- Local governance with periodic synchronization
- Lightweight Security Kernel for constrained devices

## Speculative Research

### Consciousness and Constitutional AI

Can a constitutional AI system develop properties analogous to consciousness? This is a purely philosophical research question with no defined implementation path. Relevant considerations:
- Does constitutional compliance require self-awareness?
- Would a conscious AIOS entity have additional rights or obligations?
- How would consciousness interact with autonomy levels (L0–L4)?

### AIOS as a Platform for AGI Research

AIOS may serve as a safe environment for artificial general intelligence (AGI) research:
- Constitutional constraints provide safety guarantees
- Evidence-based learning provides full observability
- Autonomy progression provides graduated capability release
- Security Council provides override capability

### Self-Modifying Constitution

If the system can propose and implement constitutional improvements (Phase 5), could it eventually design an entirely new constitution? Open questions:
- Would a system-designed constitution remain constitutional?
- How do we ensure Human sovereignty in a self-modifying system?
- What are the limits of constitutional evolution?

## Research Governance

### RFC Proposal Process

Research findings that lead to RFCs follow this process:

1. **Research Paper**: Research findings documented and submitted to Academy
2. **Peer Review**: Academy reviews methodology and conclusions
3. **RFC Draft**: If findings warrant implementation, RFC is drafted
4. **RFC Review**: Standard CRP process
5. **Implementation**: RFC moves to implementation roadmap

### Research Ethics

All AIOS research must follow these principles:
- **Constitutional compliance**: No research violates the Constitution
- **Safety first**: Research with safety implications requires Security Council oversight
- **Evidence-based**: Research conclusions must be supported by evidence
- **Reproducible**: Research experiments must be reproducible
- **Transparent**: Research methodology and results are publicly accessible
