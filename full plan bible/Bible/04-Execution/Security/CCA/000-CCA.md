## Purpose

The Capability Certification Authority (CCA) is the constitutional authority that certifies an entity's capabilities before those capabilities are accepted by the verification pipeline. CCA owns **Stage 5** of the Security Council's 7-stage Verification Pipeline — capability verification — and is the system of record for which capabilities an entity (Worker, Organization, Engine, Runtime) is authorized to exercise.

Where IDS establishes *identity*, ATS establishes *authentication*, AZS establishes *authorization* (Stages 3–4), and CCA establishes *capability certification*: the cryptographic, attestable proof that an entity's declared capabilities are genuine, within bounds, and untampered. No capability is exercised without a valid CCA certification.

## Architecture

### Relationship to the Verification Pipeline

```
Stage 1 Identity  → Stage 2 AuthN → Stage 3 AuthZ (AZS) → Stage 4 Policy (AZS/PVE)
                                                        → Stage 5 Capability Certification (CCA) ← owns
                                                        → Stage 6 Risk → Stage 7 Execution Authorization (EAS)
```

| Component | Responsibility |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Certifier** | Issues, renews, and revokes capability certificates; signs them with the CCA private key |
| **Capability Graph** | Directed graph of capability grants, delegations, and revocations (backed by CDG) |
| **Attestation Verifier** | Validates hardware/runtime attestations that back a capability claim |
| **CDC** | Capability Dependency Checker — resolves whether a requested capability chain is satisfiable |

### 5 Invariants

1. **Certification Before Exercise**: No capability is exercised without a valid, unexpired CCA certificate. (Physics/007-Capabilities.md)
2. **Single Authority**: CCA is the sole issuer of capability certificates; no other component may mint them.
3. **Bounded Delegation**: A delegated capability is bounded by its grantor's certificate; delegation depth is enforced and limited.
4. **Revocation Wins**: A revoked capability certificate is rejected by every stage downstream, even with valid signature and expiry.
5. **Attestation Required**: Capabilities that depend on a runtime/engine require a valid attestation before certification.

## Cross-Cutting Concerns

### Security

CCA is the trust anchor for capability exercise. All certificates are signed asymmetrically; the CCA private key is held in SSM and never leaves the secure boundary. (Physics/008-Security.md)

### Evidence

Every certification, revocation, and attestation produces a `CCA.*` Event recorded in the Event Store for full auditability.

### Lifecycle

Capability certificates follow Issue → Active → Revoked/Expired. Revocation is immediate and propagated to AZS, EAS, and all Runtime engines.

### Capability Bounds

A certified capability is bounded by the certifying grant chain and the entity's declared bounds from IDS/AGS. No certificate exceeds its grantor's authority. (Physics/007-Capabilities.md)
