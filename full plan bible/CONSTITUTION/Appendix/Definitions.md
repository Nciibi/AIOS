# Purpose

This document establishes the official definitions of constitutional terms used throughout AIOS.

Its purpose is to provide a single authoritative vocabulary, ensuring that every document within the AIOS ecosystem uses consistent terminology.

Definitions contained in this appendix are normative unless explicitly overridden by a constitutional amendment.

---

# Scope

This appendix applies to

- DNA
- Constitution
- Architecture Documents
- RFCs
- ADRs
- SDK Documentation
- Bible Documentation
- Future Specifications

---

# Usage

Every constitutional document SHALL use the definitions established in this appendix.

New terminology SHALL be added to this appendix before becoming part of the constitutional vocabulary.

Multiple definitions for the same constitutional term SHALL NOT exist.

# Definitions

---

## Academy

The Constitutional Institution responsible for preserving, validating, organizing and evolving the permanent knowledge of AIOS.

The Academy learns.

It does not execute.

---

## AGS (AI Genome System)

The Constitutional Institution responsible for defining, validating and managing reusable constitutional genomes.

AGS defines blueprints.

It does not instantiate them.

---

## AIOS

An Artificial Intelligence Operating System governed by a constitutional architecture that separates authority, responsibility and execution into independent institutions.

AIOS exists to coordinate intelligent systems under Human authority.

---

## ACF (AI Communication Fabric)

The Constitutional Infrastructure responsible for transporting communication between Constitutional Entities.

ACF transports communication.

It does not interpret intent.

---

## AOM (AI Object Model)

The Constitutional Infrastructure responsible for defining common object structures, relationships and metadata shared across AIOS.

AOM defines structure.

It does not govern behavior.

---

## AOP (AI Observability Platform)

The Constitutional Infrastructure responsible for collecting metrics, logs, traces and operational observations throughout AIOS.

AOP observes.

It does not influence execution.

---

## Constitutional Entity

Any entity formally recognized by the Constitution as possessing defined identity, responsibilities and constitutional protections.

Examples include

- Institutions
- Organizations
- Departments
- Workers
- Missions

---

## Constitutional Institution

A permanent constitutional component possessing exclusive constitutional responsibilities.

Institutions form the government of AIOS.

---

## Department

A permanent functional specialization unit within an Organization.

Departments preserve Functional Intelligence.

---

## DNA

The foundational architectural vision from which the Constitution derives.

DNA defines philosophy.

The Constitution defines law.

---

## DTS (Digital Twin System)

The Constitutional Institution responsible for predictive simulation and evidence-based strategic analysis.

DTS predicts.

It does not execute.

---

## Functional Intelligence

Knowledge owned by a Department concerning its specialized domain.

Functional Intelligence belongs to the Department rather than the Organization or Worker.

---

## Genome

A reusable constitutional blueprint describing how a Constitutional Entity is defined.

Genomes describe.

They do not execute.

---

## Human

The ultimate constitutional authority within AIOS.

All constitutional authority originates from Human intent.

---

## Institution

A permanent constitutional body responsible for one or more exclusive constitutional capabilities.

---

## Lifecycle

The constitutionally governed sequence of states through which a Constitutional Entity progresses during its existence.

---

## Mission

A constitutional objective that coordinates Organizations, Departments and Workers toward a defined outcome.

A Mission expresses purpose.

Execution fulfills that purpose.

---

## Operational Intelligence

Knowledge accumulated by an Organization through operational experience.

Operational Intelligence belongs to the Organization.

---

## Organization

A permanent constitutional society responsible for coordinating specialized execution within a defined operational domain.

Organizations endure.

Workers execute.

---

## OSYS (Organization System)

The Constitutional Institution responsible for creating, governing and evolving Organizations.

OSYS organizes.

It does not define strategy.

---

## ROS (Resource Operating System)

The Constitutional Institution responsible for allocating, governing and optimizing computational and physical resources.

ROS allocates.

It does not define missions.

---

## Runtime

The execution environment used by Workers to perform work.

Examples include

- Claude
- GPT
- Qwen
- DeepSeek
- Local Models

Runtimes are replaceable.

Workers remain constitutionally consistent.

---

## Runtime Neutrality

The constitutional principle that Workers, Organizations and Missions remain independent of any specific runtime implementation.

---

## Security Kernel

The Constitutional Institution responsible for authorization, policy enforcement, identity verification and constitutional trust.

The Security Kernel authorizes.

It never executes.

---

## Skill

A reusable capability that may be assigned to a Worker for the duration of a Mission.

Skills are temporary operational capabilities.

---

## Society

The collection of Constitutional Entities that cooperate under constitutional governance.

Society consists of Institutions, Organizations, Departments, Workers and Missions.

---

## Sou

The Constitutional Strategic Intelligence of AIOS.

Sou transforms Human intent into constitutional strategy.

Sou plans.

It does not execute.

---

## Worker

A temporary Constitutional Entity instantiated to execute work on behalf of an Organization during one or more Missions.

Workers execute.

They do not define strategy.

---

# Normative Language

The following keywords are interpreted according to constitutional intent.

| Keyword | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| SHALL | Mandatory requirement |
| SHALL NOT | Absolute prohibition |
| SHOULD | Strong recommendation |
| SHOULD NOT | Strong recommendation against |
| MAY | Optional behavior |
| RECOMMENDED | Preferred implementation |
| OPTIONAL | Not required |

# Future Definitions

Future constitutional revisions may introduce additional terminology.

Existing definitions SHALL remain stable whenever practical to preserve constitutional consistency across versions.

# Final Statement

A shared constitutional vocabulary is essential for architectural consistency.

This appendix establishes the common language through which every constitutional document, institutional specification and implementation guide within AIOS shall be interpreted.
