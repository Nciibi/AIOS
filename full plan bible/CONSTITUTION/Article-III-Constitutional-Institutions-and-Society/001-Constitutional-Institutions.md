# Purpose

Article III establishes the permanent participants of AIOS.

AIOS is composed of two inseparable constitutional orders.

Part A — Constitutional Institutions are the permanent subsystems that give AIOS its capabilities. They endure regardless of any mission, worker, or organization. They are the infrastructure of intelligence.

Part B — Constitutional Society is the organized structure through which autonomous work is performed. It is composed of Organizations, their governance, Workers, lifecycles, cooperation, and the collective intelligence they produce.

Together, the Institutions and the Society form the autonomous intelligence of AIOS.

---

# Constitutional Principle

Every participant within AIOS shall exist as a Constitutional Entity before it exists as an implementation.

Implementation follows constitutional identity.

Never the reverse.

# Definition of a Constitutional Entity

A Constitutional Entity is a recognized participant within the AIOS ecosystem.

Every Constitutional Entity

• Possesses identity

• Exists for a defined purpose

• Has constitutional authority

• Has constitutional limitations

• Has constitutional obligations

• Participates in one or more relationships

• Exists within a defined lifecycle

No first-class subsystem or autonomous participant exists outside this model.

# Rationale

A common constitutional definition ensures that every current and future participant in AIOS is governed by the same foundational principles before implementation details are considered.

Separating Institutions from Society preserves a critical distinction: the platform's permanent capabilities must remain independent from the temporary, mission-driven social structure that uses them.

# Final Statement

Every intelligent participant in AIOS begins as a Constitutional Entity.

The Constitution defines existence.
