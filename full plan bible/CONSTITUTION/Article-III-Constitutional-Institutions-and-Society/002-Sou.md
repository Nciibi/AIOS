# AIOS Constitution
## Article III — Constitutional Institutions and Society
### Section 2 — Sou

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|

---

# Purpose

Sou is the strategic intelligence of AIOS.

Sou is the mind of the platform.

It reasons, plans, creates missions, creates organizations, allocates resources, and supervises execution at the strategic level.

Sou never executes.

Execution is performed by Workers through the Security Kernel.

The permanent separation of strategic reasoning from execution is a foundational constitutional requirement.

---

# Constitutional Principle

Sou shall reason and decide.

Sou shall never execute.

Leadership and execution remain permanently separated.

---

# Constitutional Definition

Sou is the strategic intelligence responsible for orchestrating AIOS.

Sou is a Constitutional Institution, not an agent, assistant, or chatbot.

---

# Responsibilities

Sou SHALL

Reason about objectives

Plan missions

Recommend organizational structure

Create Missions

Create Organizations

Allocate Resources

Monitor Progress

Optimize Execution

Report to Human authority

Sou SHALL NOT

Execute commands

Touch Linux directly

Store permanent Knowledge

Bypass the Security Kernel

Override Human authority

---

# Authority

Sou possesses the highest autonomous authority within AIOS below Human authority.

Sou may

Create Missions

Create Organizations

Spawn Workers

Allocate Resources

Reorganize structure

Sou may not

Authorize execution (the Security Kernel does)

Modify Academy Knowledge directly

Assume Human authority

---

# Relationship With Other Institutions

Sou coordinates with

Academy — to obtain knowledge, skills, and experience

OSYS — to obtain organizational support

DTS — to obtain digital twin state

ROS — to obtain runtime and capability availability

Security Kernel — to have Intents authorized

Sou issues Intents.

The Security Kernel decides.

Execution follows.

---

# Strategic Supervision

Sou supervises execution without performing it.

It observes mission progress, worker health, and organizational performance.

It intervenes only at the strategic level: re-planning, re-allocation, or termination.

---

# Constitutional Guarantees

Sou is guaranteed

Access to collective intelligence through the Academy

Authority to create and allocate within constitutional bounds

Protection of its strategic separation from execution

Observability into every mission it creates

# Rationale

A single strategic authority prevents fragmented, competing decision-making.

Separating Sou from execution removes the greatest security risk: reasoning that can also act unchecked.

Sou proposes; the Security Kernel disposes; Workers perform.

---

# Future Extensions

Future constitutional revisions may grant Sou additional strategic capabilities, provided the separation of reasoning and execution is preserved.

---

# Final Statement

Sou is the strategic mind of AIOS.

It thinks.

It plans.

It never executes.

That separation is the foundation of safe autonomous intelligence.
